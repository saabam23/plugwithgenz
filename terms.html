✰ TERMS 2 KNOW ✰

Double tap on each term to learn!

Also called, Major Depressive Disorder or depression, is an ongoing low mood and loss of interest in activities a person used to find enjoyable; to be confirmed as MDD, symptoms must be present everyday for at least two weeks.

Long lasting form of depression; symptoms are present on most days for at least two years.

Depression that occurs after childbirth; There is no single cause of postpartum depression but physical and emotional issues can play a role.

Sudden episodes of intense fear or panic that triggers severe physical symptoms- may be in reaction to traumatic events, dangerous experiences, or out of the blue. Symptoms include heavy breathing, increased heart rate, dizziness, headache, stomach pain, chest pain, trembling, sweating, etc.

A chronic condition that includes difficulty in sustaining attention, hyperactivity, and impulsive behavior. Women are sometimes underdiagnosed if there is an occurrence of anxiety or depression along with ADHD, the symptoms might be mistaken.

A wide variety of disorders characterized by “dissociation”, or a disconnect between your mind and body. Dissociation may feel like an out-of-body experience, often caused by traumatic events. Treatment includes therapy, meditation, hypnosis, and medication.

A severe separation from reality that is caused by multiple symptoms, including paranoia, disorganized behavior, delusions, and hallucinations. They may be caused by genetics, stress, drug and alcohol use, etc. Treatment includes therapy and medication.

Mental disorder characterized by a skewed perception of reality. Symptoms include delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech, and negative personal hygiene. Requires permanent treatment through therapy and medication.

Negative perception of your personality or physical attributes.

Phobias are drastic and irrational fears that may result from past trauma. To some, they are crippling–those with agoraphobia have an intense fear of others and the unfamiliar. Others may avoid situations they enjoy because of their phobias. *Phobias do not have to be “rational”, common fears such as spiders or heights. They vary from Thalassophobia (fear of the deep ocean) to Sidonglobophobia (fear of cotton balls)!

A mood disorder that occurs depending on the varying levels of sunlight usually during fall and winter months.

Intense thoughts related to the experience or witnessing of a traumatic event; symptoms may include nightmares, flashbacks, anxiety and constant thought about the event.

When a person constantly worries about an intrusive thought that cause irresistible behaviors; these behaviors are performed to relieve the anxiety associated with the obsessive thoughts

Bipolar disorder is marked by extreme changes in mood, thought, energy and behavior. Individuals with bipolar disorder switch between symptoms of mania and depression. There are 3 types of bipolar disorder: Bipolar 1 disorder, Bipolar 2 disorder, and cyclothymia. It is also very difficult to diagnose.

Hyperfocusing on physical flaws you believe you possess- to the point of impaired perception, or seeing things that aren’t true. Treatment includes CBT (cognitive behavioral therapy) +/ medication

A mental disorder characterized by a difficulty to regulate emotions. People with BPD may become dependent on someone else whilst lashing out, acting impulsively, and maintaining a fear of abandonment. Causes vary, including genetics, chemical imbalances, and abuse or trauma. Therapy is a common treatment, and can be paired with medication if necessary. Some may think BPD is the same as Bipolar Disorder, and while they may share certain symptoms, they are entirely different and should be regarded as such.

An inability to move your body as you are waking up, caused by an incongruence between your mind’s awake state and your body’s sleep state. May be paired with vivid visions, named “hypnopompic hallucinations”.

Anxiety caused by social situations, being in public, interacting with others. May be treated through medication and therapy, especially exposure therapy- symptoms may decline with age.

Disordered state resulting from disturbing events- may be caused by anything emotionally stressful. Trauma, especially repeated trauma, may result in a plethora of mental disorders.

An umbrella term for a multitude of different disorders that center around body image and food intake. Among these are Anorexia Nervosa (extreme food avoidance), Bulimia Nervosa (binging and purging), Binge Eating Disorder (extreme food consumption), and ARFID (Avoidant Restrictive Food Intake Disorder). Eating Disorders are most often caused by societal pressure, abuse, and genetics. They can be treated through therapy and by consulting dietitians. It is often said that everyone who has an Eating Disorder must be thin; in reality, negative body image comes in all shapes and sizes. This stereotype often leads to the dismissal of potential symptoms in those who are genuinely sick.
